Common Procedures

Bone Grafting

Bone grafting rebuilds jawbone support where volume has been lost after infection, trauma, or missing teeth. This procedure creates a stronger foundation for future implant placement.

We evaluate your site with 3D imaging and select a grafting approach tailored to your timeline, anatomy, and long-term treatment goals.

Why Is Bone Grafting Needed?

  • Prevents further bone loss after tooth extraction
  • Rebuilds jawbone structure for dental implants
  • Restores facial shape and support
  • Improves overall oral health and function

Procedure Overview

Bone grafting is typically performed under local anesthesia. The grafting material is placed in the area of bone loss, where it acts as a scaffold for new bone growth. Over time, your natural bone integrates with the graft, strengthening the area.

Recovery & Healing

  • Initial healing takes a few days to a week
  • Full bone regeneration may take 3-6 months
  • Mild swelling or discomfort is normal and temporary
  • Following aftercare instructions is essential for success
